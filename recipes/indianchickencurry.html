Indian Chicken Curry

Description

Indian chicken curry, a beloved staple in households across the country, is a tantalizing symphony of flavors and aromas that dance on the palate. Tender pieces of chicken are simmered in a rich and fragrant gravy, infused with a vibrant blend of spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The curry's color, ranging from fiery red to golden yellow, reflects the unique combination of spices and ingredients used in its preparation. Each region of India boasts its own distinctive version of chicken curry, showcasing the diverse culinary landscape of the country.

Whether it's the creamy and mild butter chicken of the north, the fiery vindaloo of Goa, or the coconut-infused curries of the south, Indian chicken curry is a testament to the country's rich culinary heritage. The dish is often served with fluffy basmati rice or warm naan bread, allowing one to savor every l ast drop of the flavorful gravy. Beyond its delectable taste, chicken curry also holds cultural significance in India, often gracing special occasions and family gatherings. It is a dish that brings people together, fostering a sense of community and shared enjoyment.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ teaspoon grated fresh ginger root
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • salt to taste
  • 2 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • ¾ cup coconut milk
  • ½ lemon, juiced
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion until lightly browned.
  2. Stir in garlic, curry powder, cinnamon, paprika, bay leaf, ginger, sugar, and salt. Continue stirring for 2 minutes.
  3. Add chicken pieces, tomato paste, yogurt, and coconut milk. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes.
  4. Remove bay leaf, and stir in lemon juice and cayenne pepper. Simmer 5 more minutes.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
