Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

Description

A teriyaki salmon bowl is a delicious and healthy dish that combines the rich flavors of salmon with the sweet and savory notes of teriyaki sauce. he salmon is typically marinated in a teriyaki mixture made from soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar, then grilled or pan-seared to perfection. The glaze caramelizes on the salmon, giving it a slightly crispy exterior while keeping the fish tender and juicy inside. This dish is often served on a bed of fluffy rice, absorbing the flavorful teriyaki sauce and making each bite satisfying.

In addition to the salmon and rice, a teriyaki salmon bowl is commonly paired with a variety of fresh vegetables, such as steamed broccoli, edamame, or pickled ginger, adding color and texture to the dish. Sometimes, a sprinkling of sesame seeds or a drizzle of extra teriyaki sauce enhances the flavors further. This Bowl is not only a visually appealing meal but also a balanced one, offering protein, carbs, and vitamins all in one bowl, making it a perfect choice for a nutritious lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 small clove garlic, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger
  • 1 (6 ounce) salmon filet
  • 1/4 cup grated carrots
  • 2 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded red cabbage
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1 green onion, thinly sliced (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C) and spray a baking dish with cooking spray.
  2. Combine soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl. Place the salmon in the prepared baking dish, skin side down. Pour teriyaki sauce over.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until fish flakes easily with a fork, 12 to 15 minutes.
  4. Place rice in a bowl, top with salmon, and spoon over teriyaki sauce from the baking dish. Add in the shredded cabbage, grated carrots, and radishes. Sprinkle with sliced green onions and sesame seeds, to garnish.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
